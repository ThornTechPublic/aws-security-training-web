Module 1: Initial Connection Instructions

Objectives

  • Connect to your AWS account using Single-Sign On (SSO)
  • Provision individual IAM accounts
  • Set Group permissions for these IAM accounts

Overview

The class will be divided into "one-pizza" teams of approximately 3 members each. Each team will have its own AWS account to perform the labs throughout this course.

In order to access this AWS account, each team will be given credentials to a Single-Sign On account. This SSO account has full administrator privileges.

You will not be using the SSO account to perform the labs. Rather, you will use the SSO account to provision individual IAM accounts for each group member.

Nominate one member of your group to perform the steps in this lab. Other team members are encouraged to follow along, or read the "Things to note" section at the bottom of this lab while you're waiting.

Log into SSO account

Go to https://thorntechtraining.awsapps.com/start

You will see the SSO login page.

Enter the username and password provided to your team.

You should see the following screen:

  • Click on the big orange cube to expand the list.
  • Expand your AWS account (e.g. 453832482648 userone)
  • Next to AWSAdministratorAccess, click Management console
  • On the top right, switch your region to ​N. Virginia
  • You're in! Move on to the next lab.

Things to note

Single-Sign On

Many AWS environments do not use Single-Sign On. Typically, you will see the IAM login flow. We are using Single-Sign On in a group lab setting to help manage multiple AWS accounts.

Service limits

In this course, teams are limited to just a handful of members. This is to prevent running into service limits. For example, there's a soft limit of 5 VPCs per AWS account. So everyone needs to be diligent about deleting resources when instructed to do so, in order to prevent hitting these service limits.

Code of conduct

The goal is for everyone to learn and get some hands on practice with AWS. Please try your best to avoid doing anything that would disrupt others from performing the labs.

Note that some public labs like QwikLabs are locked down so tightly that your AWS console experience is no longer realistic. In these labs, you are given root level access so you can troubleshoot and get through content as quickly as possible. Please do not abuse this privilege.

Troubleshooting

If SSO keeps kicking you out, try using a guest browser profile in Chrome, in case something is cached.
